Spatial Sampling & Interpolation of Environmental Data

Spatial Statistics & Raster Analysis | Sept 2025

Technical Implementation

  • Designed and implemented systematic, random, and stratified random sampling strategies to extract elevation values from a DEM, comparing sampling bias and mean estimates across methods using consistent spatial extents and scales.
  • Constructed stratification zones from slope-derived rasters, applying majority and focal filtering to generalize terrain classes and allocating samples proportionally based on polygon area and weighted importance.
  • Generated continuous environmental surfaces using multiple interpolation techniques—Thiessen polygons, Inverse Distance Weighting (IDW), Kernel interpolation, and Kriging—evaluating model performance through cross-validation diagnostics (RMSE, residuals, predicted vs. observed plots).
  • Produced multi-panel analytical map layouts to compare sampling schemes and interpolation results side-by-side, clearly communicating uncertainty, smoothing behavior, and spatial prediction differences.
